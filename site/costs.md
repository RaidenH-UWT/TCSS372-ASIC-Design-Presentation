There are many variables to consider when designing an ASIC chip. One of the major advantages of
ASICs is that they benefit from economies of scale, having a high upfront cost for designing the
chip and building the machines to manufacture a specific ASIC, but being very cheap to mass
produce after that.

During the upfront design process, there are more parameters to consider. Complexity is a major
one, with ASIC complexity massively increasing as the technology gets more powerful, it becomes
harder and harder to design an ASIC that takes advantage of the technological capabilities. This
cost can be mitigated by taking advantage of preexisting designs, but this may impact performance
because these designs aren't necessarily optimized for the purpose of this ASIC

Once the upfront costs are through, there's the unit costs to consider. These are much smaller,
made so by the economy of scale, but are still important to consider. There are two main costs
that are relevant here, the manufacture cost (the cost of manufacturing a single unit) and the
power requirements (how much energy the ASIC takes to run).
