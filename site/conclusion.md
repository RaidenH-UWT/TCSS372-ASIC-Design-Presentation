Now, we've learned some of the process behind how ASICs are designed and why.

They benefit from from economies of scale, which make them expensive to design but very cheap
to mass produce, making them viable products to sell.

We've learned that during the design process there are many variables that must be weighed against 
each other. Higher complexity increases design costs, but can lead to increased efficiency. More 
programmability can lead one ASIC to be more multipurpose, but can be more expensive and less efficient.

We've learned that ASIC circuits are designed at four levels, from circuits at the very bottom,
built all the way up to the full system.

All of these have come together to explain why ASICs are found all over the place, from cars
to thermometers to microphones. ASICs serve an important role in modern computing, allowing many
different kinds of devices to be integrated with current digital technology to be made smarter
and more efficient.
